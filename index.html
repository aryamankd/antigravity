Watch Dictionary

The Collector's Glossary

Essential watch terminology from A to Z.

Automatic

+

A mechanical watch that winds itself using a rotor — a semicircular weight that spins with the motion of your wrist. Also called "self-winding."

Annual Calendar

+

A complication that displays day, date, and month, automatically accounting for months with 30 or 31 days. Only requires one manual correction per year.

Appliqué

+

Hour markers or indices that are separately manufactured and then attached to the dial, rather than printed. Typically made of metal.

Amplitude

+

The arc (in degrees) that the balance wheel swings through during each oscillation. A healthy watch typically has 250°–310°.

AR Coating

+

Anti-reflective coating applied to the crystal to reduce glare and improve legibility. Can be applied to one or both sides.

Bezel

+

The ring surrounding the crystal. Can be fixed or rotating, with markings for timing, GMT, or tachymeter.

Balance Wheel

+

The oscillating component at the heart of a mechanical movement that regulates timekeeping at typically 28,800 vibrations per hour.

Blued Hands

+

Watch hands heat-treated to ~300°C until they turn deep blue. A hallmark of fine finishing in haute horlogerie.

Bracelet

+

A metal band of individual links. Styles include Oyster, Jubilee, and President.

Bridges

+

Metal plates holding movement components. Often decorated with Geneva stripes, perlage, or anglage.

Chronograph

+

A built-in stopwatch function with sub-dials tracking elapsed seconds, minutes, and hours. One of the most popular complications.

Caliber

+

The specific movement identified by number or name. "In-house" calibers are designed by the brand itself — a mark of prestige.

Complication

+

Any function beyond hours, minutes, and seconds — date, chronograph, GMT, moon phase, power reserve. "Grand complications" combine multiple.

Crown

+

The knob for winding and setting the time. Screw-down crowns thread into the case for water resistance.

COSC

+

Official Swiss chronometer testing institute. Certified watches must keep time within -4/+6 seconds per day over 15 days of testing.
